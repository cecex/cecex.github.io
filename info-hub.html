Info Hub Mobile App

UX/UI Design, User Research

Design Proposal for Design Exchange Connect:ONTrack Competition
Collaborators: Garth Duncan, Michael Kozlowski, & Simon Madadi


We were invited to submit proposals to Design Exchange that imagine barrier-free features for public transportation in Ontario that are accessible to the greatest number of individuals.

Concept

We are proposing an app paired with digital displays that will allow Canadians with hearing impairment issues who use GO train services to access information in a barrier free way. By alerting users through their mobile devices and digital display, hearing impaired individuals will be able to see the information that is exclusively given to GO train passengers through auditory announcements.

Summary

Our proposed design concept, Info Hub, is aimed to help hearing impaired individuals who currently use or plan to use GO Transit services. Through our proposed concept, users with hearing barriers will be able to comfortably and confidently ride the GO Train by being alerted to the same information that other passengers regularly receive through overhead speakers.

Our proposed design is both an application for users mobile devices and a overhead digital display that will be featured both at the station and in GO train cars. Passengers will be able to see the information that is being announced to passengers via loudspeaker (Track delays or changes, current and upcoming stops, emergency information) on both their phone and digital display screens - whichever is their preference. This eliminates the feeling of being lost, confused and anxious for hearing impaired individuals riding the GO Train as the information is now that their fingertips and barrier-free.

By having the information displayed on screens throughout the station and on train cars, hearing impaired individuals are able to keep their dignity in regards to exposing their disability to strangers and do not need to ask for additional help for common issues faced (i.e.; not knowing what stop they are at, why the train is delayed, when they will be arriving, what is the new track number, etc.).

In addition to the overhead digital displays, hearing impaired individuals will also be able to access this information via their mobile devices. In this app, users will be able to set a notification/phone vibration for when the GO train has arrived at their stop, as well as get in contact with a GO Transit employee if they require additional assistance.

Without the use of our proposed design, hearing impaired passengers are alienated and often left without the necessary information to successfully complete their ride on their own.
