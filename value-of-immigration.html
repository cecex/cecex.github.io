Value of Immigration Data Visualization

UX/UI Design, User Research

School Project supported by Government of Canada's Central Innovation Hub
Collaborator: Simon Madadi


We were challenged to find a way to address societal issues while working with and meeting the requirements set by the Innovation Hub of Canada. My team member and I decided on targeting the issue of: How might we highlight the importance of Immigration in Canada?

Process

This project was broken into phases which resulted in a research report, data visualization mega map and accompanying website. The driver for our project was the fact: In a national polling partnership between CBC and the Angus Reid Institute, 68% of Canadian participants said minorities should be “doing more to fit in with mainstream society instead of keeping their own customs and languages”. We found this fact polarizing and driven from an uneducated perspective on immigration, so we worked towards finding solutions on addressing this issue.

Research

Our research explored the past, current and predicted statistics of Canadian immigration, how Canadian born citizens perceive immigration, the Mandate Letter written to the Minister of Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship from Prime Minister Trudeau, in order to gather data for our future visualizations. In addition to this research, we conducted interviews and read accounts with those who have immigrated to Canada and the challenges they faced upon arrival. From this research, we were able to create an empathy map, value proposition, journey map, user personas, a projected timeline and implementation plan based on our findings.

Data Synthesis

We began to conceptualize all of our research into data visualizations that would make sense in a finished product and read well. We brainstormed and decided on our objectives and how we could best implement our 'how might' question.

Mega Map

Our 4ft x 3ft mega map contains all the information we felt was necessary to inform our target audience about the value of immigration. The left side of the map shows current and projected statistics of Canadian Immigration. The top right section explains how immigration helps with the development of Canada and continues to make it a thriving country. The bottom right section shows ways that Canadians can help change the stigma surrounding Canadian Immigration.
